How Waythrough works

Most people facing legal or financial situations have no idea where to start. Waythrough cuts through the confusion in three simple steps.

Step 1 — Describe your situation

Speak your situation out loud, type it in your own words, or photograph the letter or document. Waythrough accepts voice input, text, images and PDFs — whatever is easiest when you're stressed.

  • No legal jargon required — just explain what happened
  • Upload a photo of the letter directly
  • Voice-to-text works in 20+ languages

Step 2 — Get clear guidance

Waythrough's AI analyses your situation and gives you a plain-English breakdown including what it means, how urgent it is, what to do next, common mistakes to avoid, and questions to ask a professional.

  • Urgency rating: low, medium or high
  • Deadline detection — if there's a time limit, we flag it
  • Step-by-step action plan
  • Local resources and helplines for your country

Step 3 — Ask follow-up questions

Still confused about something? Tap "Ask a follow-up" to have a conversation about your specific situation. Waythrough remembers the context of what you've shared and gives tailored answers.

  • Ask anything about your situation
  • Save guidance to refer back to later
  • Copy and share guidance with family or advisers

Frequently asked questions

Is Waythrough free to use?
Yes — Waythrough is completely free. The service is supported by advertising. There are no subscriptions, no hidden fees, and no limits on how many times you can use it.
Is the guidance legal advice?
No. Waythrough provides educational information and guidance only — not formal legal or financial advice. We help you understand your situation and know what steps to take, but we always recommend consulting a qualified professional for complex matters.
How accurate is the guidance?
Waythrough uses Claude AI by Anthropic, one of the most capable AI systems available. The guidance is tailored to your specific country's laws and is reviewed for accuracy. However, laws change and individual circumstances vary — always verify important information with a qualified professional.
Is my information private?
Yes. The situations you describe are processed to generate your guidance but are not stored on our servers. Your saved history is kept only on your device. We do not sell or share your personal information. Read our full privacy policy for details.
What if I get a final demand letter?
Don't panic — a final demand letter is serious but not the end of the road. Open Waythrough, select "Debt & Bills", describe the letter or photograph it, and get immediate guidance on what to do next, including local helplines and free advice services in your country.
What employment rights do I have if I'm made redundant?
Redundancy rights vary significantly by country. In the UK you may be entitled to statutory redundancy pay, a notice period, and the right to appeal. In the UAE, MOHRE regulations govern end-of-service benefits. In the US, there is no federal law requiring severance. Waythrough gives you country-specific guidance based on your situation.
Can my landlord evict me before my tenancy ends?
In most countries, a landlord cannot legally evict you before your contract ends without specific legal grounds. In the UK, they must follow Section 21 or Section 8 procedures. In the UAE, eviction notices must follow RERA regulations. Use Waythrough to get specific guidance for your country and situation.
I received an HMRC/tax authority letter. What should I do?
Do not ignore tax authority letters. Most have strict response deadlines. Use Waythrough to photograph or describe the letter and get immediate guidance on what type of letter it is, how urgent it is, and what your next steps should be. Free tax advice services like TaxAid (UK) may also be able to help.
